Ecosystem Overview

PISTA

A multi-business venue where padel, dining, wellness, and fitness operate under one roof — connected by a single guest platform powered by Sarnies.

Core 01

System

Architecture

How a Business Plugs In

Any business at the venue can partner up. They plug in and become part of the ecosystem instantly — gaining access to shared guests, loyalty, payments, and bookings.

What the Sarnies Platform Covers

The shared service layer. The Sarnies Platform facilitates and connects — every business at the venue benefits from:

Guest Check-In

One profile per guest across all businesses. Check-in, wristband assignment, session tracking from arrival to departure.

Bookings & Scheduling

Courts, spa sessions, gym classes, any reservable service. Guests book once, access with their wristband.

Tap Payments / POS Integration

Guests tap to pay at any business. One tab, multiple vendors, settled at checkout. Bridges different POS systems seamlessly.

Loyalty

Cross-business loyalty that connects all venues. Points, perks, tiers — the system that makes guests come back.

Access Rules

Who goes where. Capacity limits. Age-based restrictions. Booking requirements. Platform-defined, hardware-enforced.

Accounting & Reporting

Unified transaction view. Each business partner sees their own data. Point exchange ledger, business reporting, audit trail.

Vendor Categories

Vendors are components, not foundations. Grouped by function. Any can be replaced without affecting the core.

1. Access & Identity Hardware

NFC wristbands, readers, gates, lockers. Any NFC-compatible hardware works — identity lives in the platform.

2. Booking & Scheduling

Courts, spa, gym classes, any reservable service. Platform syncs via adapter — swapping the system changes the adapter, nothing else.

3. Point of Sale

Order management, kitchen display, inventory. Different businesses can use different POS systems — the platform bridges them.

4. Payment Processing

Card processing, PromptPay, pay-as-you-go. An API call away — switching providers is a config change.

5. Loyalty / CRM

Each business can run their own loyalty program. Optionally connects to the Sarnies Platform for cross-business rewards.

6. Accounting & Reporting

Transaction logs, point exchange ledger, business reporting. Unified view across the ecosystem.

Technical Feasibility

Intentionally high-level. The purpose is to demonstrate the platform is buildable — not to define how.

Guest Layer

Mobile app + wristband. App for booking, viewing tab. Band for everything on-site.

Platform Core

Identity, sessions, payments, loyalty, access, accounting. Cloud-hosted with local fallback.

Business Adapters

Connectors to each business partner's systems. New business = new adapter, not new platform.

Hardware Layer

NFC readers, gates, kiosks, lockers. Works offline. Staff override at every point.

Offline Resilience

  • Full connectivity: Everything real-time, cloud-synced.
  • Internet down: Local server handles access, tabs, sessions. Syncs on reconnect.
  • Power outage: Battery backup on critical readers. Lockers fail-open. Staff goes manual.

Mobile Apps (Conceptual)

  • Guest app: Book courts, view tab, loyalty points, nudges, digital receipt.
  • Staff app: Check-in, manage wristbands, override access, handle exceptions, live occupancy.

Core 03

Cross-Business

The moat. What makes the ecosystem sticky and what no single vendor can replicate. A 3-layer loyalty model where every business can participate at the level that suits them.

3-Layer Loyalty Model

Layer 1
Own Loyalty (per business)

Each business runs its own points program. My customers earn my points, spend my points. Simple, no cross-business accounting needed. Optionally connect to the Sarnies Platform for cross-visibility.

Layer 2
Welcome Gifts (ecosystem-wide)

Every business agrees to give a standard welcome perk to any registered PISTA guest. 10% off first visit, free item, etc. Each business absorbs their own cost — it's their marketing spend for being in the ecosystem. The Sarnies Platform distributes the offer digitally on registration.

Layer 3
Collab Exchange (optional, between specific businesses)

Two businesses agree to a special cross-deal: "Show 500 padel points, get 20% off a meal." This needs a centralized exchange system — like a credit card network. Sarnies acts as the clearinghouse: validates points, applies the benefit. Businesses settle between themselves.

  • Sarnies provides the rails — it doesn't own the banks
  • Each deal is opt-in — businesses negotiate terms directly
  • Scalable — any two partners can create a collab at any time

What Makes This Defensible

Any venue can install wristband readers. Any cafe can run a POS. But a cross-business loyalty system that makes guests feel like the whole venue knows them — that's what keeps people coming back and what makes new businesses want to join.

Future Scale

Same loyalty works outside PISTA. Earn points here, spend at partner venues. Or another venue adopts the platform — their members join the same loyalty network.

04

Discussion Points

Internal — for us to align on before the meeting.

Known Risks

Vendor dependency. If a POS or booking system changes terms, the platform is affected. Mitigation: the Sarnies Platform abstracts vendors — swapping one doesn't break the guest experience.

Hardware failure. Readers, gates, or kiosks go down. Mitigation: offline cache + manual staff override at every touchpoint.

Check-in bottleneck. 13 sqm check-in area. Peak Saturdays = 50+ arrivals in 30 min. Mitigation: self-service kiosk, pre-registration via app, staggered slots.

3rd party adoption. Getting operators to adopt requires clear value: more customers, less tech overhead, loyalty that drives repeats.

Data & privacy. PDPA (Thailand) governs guest data. Consent required. Platform must handle cleanly.

Open Questions

Payment model. Three supported options, each with different friction and fit:
1. Pre-loaded credits — water park style, guest loads balance upfront
2. Membership billing — gym style, recurring charge covers access
3. Pay-as-you-go — tap and charge directly at each touchpoint

Wristband economics. Two sourcing paths:
Reusable silicone: ~$1.20–3.50/unit at 1,000+ qty (ShopNFC, RFIDSilicone). Premium feel, needs sanitization + inventory management.
Disposable Tyvek/fabric: ~$0.50–1.25/unit (NFCntag, Tagstand). Simpler ops, less premium, higher ongoing cost.

Minors & children. Do kids under 12 get wristbands? What zones are restricted? Age-based access can be configured per-business (e.g. bar, spa) or venue-wide. Discussion point — not a hard rule yet.
